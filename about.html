About this simulator

Romania offers several legal structures for independent IT contractors and freelancers — PFA, II, IF, and SRL — each with different tax rules, contribution thresholds, and trade-offs. Choosing between them is surprisingly hard, especially for foreigners working in Romania or Romanians returning from abroad.

Why this exists

Tax comparison tools for Romanian structures are rare, and most available information is scattered across accounting blogs, Facebook groups, and outdated forums. This simulator aims to bring clarity by letting you compare all major structures side-by-side with transparent, inspectable calculations.

How it works

The simulator uses publicly available tax rates, contribution thresholds, and rules from ANAF (the Romanian tax authority) and the Romanian Fiscal Code. All constants are sourced and marked with confidence levels.

Every result is assumption-driven. The simulator does not know your personal situation — it models scenarios based on what you enter. You can inspect the full breakdown, see which rules apply, and verify the logic yourself.

What this is not

This is not legal or tax advice. It is an estimation tool designed to help you understand the landscape and ask better questions when consulting a qualified Romanian accountant or tax advisor.

Some simplifications are made: VAT is treated as a warning only, quarterly dividend timing is modeled as an assumption, and certain edge cases (multiple CAEN codes, linked enterprises, part-year scenarios) are not covered.
